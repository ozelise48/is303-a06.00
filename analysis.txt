1. DATASET DESCRIPTION.          
This analysis utilizes the 'movie_ratings.csv' dataset, which originally contained 50 
rows. After filtering out missing data during the cleaning process, 47 rows and 6 columns 
remained for analysis. The dataset captures essential movie metrics, including the title, 
genre, platform, times watched, numerical rating (on a scale of 1 to 5), and the release 
year (originally stored as text strings). The data was sourced from the IS 303 course files 
on Canvas to evaluate how streaming platforms and genres compare in user satisfaction over 
time.

2. KEY FINDINGS
Based on the pandas groupby aggregation and the generated line chart, 
two primary trends emerged:
- First, the highest-rated genre on average was Musical with a score of 3.90, 
while Action performed the worst with an average rating of 2.77.
- Second, comparing platforms revealed that Amazon Prime maintained the highest 
average user rating (3.26), whereas Netflix unexpectedly performed the worst with a much 
lower average rating of 2.50.
- Finally, the 'ratings_over_time.png' chart indicates that average movie ratings 
fluctuate based on the release year, visualizing the historical shift in content quality 
across these distribution channels.

3. LIMITATIONS
While the analysis highlights clear patterns, it has significant limitations. 
The dataset is relatively small at just 47 rows, which can make historical averages 
highly volatile; for example, if only one or two movies represent a specific release year, 
those single ratings heavily skew that year's average on the trend line. Furthermore, 
the data lacks a 'total vote count' variable, meaning we cannot distinguish between a 
movie rated 5.0 by one person versus a movie rated 4.5 by thousands. To strengthen these 
conclusions, future analyses should incorporate larger sample sizes from public databases 
like IMDb or Kaggle and include user demographic data to control for platform target 
audiences.
